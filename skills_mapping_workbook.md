Ethnography

**What is ethnography?**

- The study of individual cultures. 

When you study the behaviour of the staff in a supermarket chain, you’re attempting to interpret and analyse how the staff talk about their outdated equipment. 

When you study the behaviour of other players in a game, what are you attempting to interpret and analyse?

**Ethnography: observing and examining behaviour in real-world and digital environments - from retail staff encountered as a field service engineer, to other players in multiplayer games - and analysing the meanings expressed through their behaviour at the systems level.**

Autoethnography

What is autoethnography?

Ethnography, but instead of looking at other people, you’re observing, examining, and analysing your own behaviour within a social situation and trying to understand what it means about the system, culture, or environment you’re part of. 

A big part of autoethnography is metacognition.
